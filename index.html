Inchang Baek

AI Researcher · Procedural Content Generation · Game AI · Reinforcement Learning

I am a Ph.D. Candidate at the AI Graduate School, Gwangju Institute of Science and Technology (GIST), under the supervision of Prof. Kyung-Joong Kim. My research focuses on Procedural Content Generation (PCG) using deep reinforcement learning and large language models, with applications in game AI, automated level design, and intelligent tutoring systems. I am particularly interested in leveraging LLMs for reward design and instruction-aware representation learning to enable more flexible and human-aligned content generation.

Interests

Research Areas

My research sits at the intersection of game AI, reinforcement learning, and large language models.

🎮

Procedural Content Generation

Designing automated systems that generate game levels, environments, and content using reinforcement learning and evolutionary algorithms. Focusing on human-aligned and instruction-following PCG via LLMs.

🤖

Deep Reinforcement Learning

Developing novel RL algorithms including offline RL data augmentation, multi-agent RL with clustering, and reward shaping techniques for complex game environments.

🧠

Large Language Models for Game AI

Leveraging LLMs (e.g., GPT) for automatic reward function design, procedural level generation guidance, and natural language-based game content creation.

📚

Intelligent Tutoring Systems

Building personalized in-game tutorial generators that adapt to player knowledge states, integrating student models with procedural context generation for seamless learning experiences.

👥

Cooperative Game AI

Investigating cooperative level generation and AI agents in multiplayer settings (e.g., Overcooked), including Turing test frameworks for evaluating human-likeness in cooperative play.

🔬

Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning

Scaling MARL to many-agent environments using clustering-based approaches, and developing evaluation methodologies for emergent cooperative behaviors in complex game scenarios.
